Home Decor Style Guide

Introduction

This is an in-depth style guide for Home Décor and will provide a clear guideline to keep the website consistent and reliable. The guideline will include how to use the brand logo, colour palette, typography, graphics, layout and placements, forms, and other interactive elements.

Brand Personality

The brand personality is warm, peaceful, comfortable, and welcoming. It loves nature and the environment, and cares for those around them. The products are focused on a Scandinavian style with light wood, white paint, green plants, and black industrial details.

Target Audience

The audience we are targeting our market for is adult women and men in their 30-60s with a wish for a warm and peaceful home to relax in.

Colour Palette

The planet is currently heating up and the environmental movement is growing faster like never before. We see the big and small companies using this towards their marketing and design. The colour green has been their symbolic colour and make people associate the colour with cleanliness, nature, harmony, and life. For this reason and for the reason Scandinavian styles uses a lot of plants in their design I have chosen a calm olive green to be my primary colour, and the colour on my logo.

Logo

The logo was created in Adobe Illustrator using the font called Berlin Sans FB, normal style. The logo is simplistic, yet warm and welcoming. The colour chosen is the brand primary colour and represent the brand personality well.

Placement

The logo should always be placed in the top left corner at all times. This is because the user will always look in that spot first.

Colours

The colour chosen is the brands primary colour. A soft olive green.

Layout

The layout consists of 3 columns for the products, a side menu for filtering categories and a top navigation for all the basic needs the user has. On tablet det layout will wrap down to two columns and on mobile down to one column where a menu icon and a filter icon will appear to toggle the different navigations.
